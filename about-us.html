    • About Us

    The Oaks are a mixed group of talented (and not so talented) ex-cricketers and first timers alike. Most members are affiliated with the official Oaks Cricket Club in some way. The age of our Players range from 18 to over 70 years. Grandsons have played with Grandfathers with little or no resulting arguments. Like all good Taverners we pride ourselves in our ability to "Talk a good game" without feeling the need to actually play a good one. The Club is a branch of the official club, which is part of the taverners league.

    Cricketing Info

    Taverners Cricket usually lasts around two and a half to three hours, with sambos and drinks after (which could last even longer!). It is usually payed in the format of 15 overs of 8 balls or just a plain T20, rules and additional overs will be discussed between the teams playing. Matches are usually played on a friday evening at 6 pm with "practise" sessions every wednesday evening. The Taverners season begins and ends at the same time of any Irish cricketing season, between the end of May to the beginning of September. To be a part of the Taverners club will be 100 Euros for the season, with the occasional pitch maintenance costs needed to keep our ground in pristene condition (usually around 25Euros a year). Cricketing equiptment is provided.

    How to Play

    The main objective in cricket is simple: The batsmen aim to score more runs than the other team, while the bowlers and fielders try to stop them. Batting Runs are scored when a batsmen hits the ball and runs to the other end of the pitch before the fielders can throw it back. If the ball is hit well it can go all the way to the boundary and the batsmen gets an automatic four runs, without having to actually run. Bowling The bowler's main job is to get the batsmen out. The bowler does this by running in and delivering the ball to the batsmen.
